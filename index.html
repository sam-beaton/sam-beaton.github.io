About Me

I am a PhD student at KCL, developing analytical pipelines for functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) and high-density diffuse optical tomography (HD-DOT) data as part of the INDiGO Trial. For these data my work has the dual aims of (i) determining optimal combinations of existing signal processing approaches and (ii) the development of novel statistical analysis techniques utilising methods from functional data analysis, a branch of mathematical statistics.

I'm interested in all things maths, neuroimaging, and brains; particularly the application of pure mathematical theory to neuroimaging data analysis to better understand neurodevelopment!

I completed an MSc in Brain Imaging at the University of Nottingham, and an MMath in Mathematics from the University of Leeds.
